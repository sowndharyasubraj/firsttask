About Mehandi

Mehandi which is also known as a shagun ki Mehandi is a form of body art or a temporary skin decoration in Indian culture which is usually drawn in legs and hands. Mehandi is commonly used by women of South Asia such as India, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Nepal, Maldives, Sri Lanka, North Africa, and the Middle East.

Mehandi art is a creative art that is a necessary part of Indian culture for festivals, weddings, and some other traditions like Karvachauth, Bhaidooj, RakshaBandhan, Durga Pua, Teej, etc. However, in the west Mehandi is simply known as henna.
